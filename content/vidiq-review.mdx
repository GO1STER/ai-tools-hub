## What vidIQ Does

vidIQ is a YouTube analytics and growth tool that installs as a browser extension and integrates with YouTube Studio. It overlays data directly onto YouTube's interface and adds a separate dashboard for deeper analytics.

Core features:
- **Keyword research** — search volume, competition scores, related terms
- **Video scorecard** — SEO strength rating for any video's title, description, and tags
- **Competitor analysis** — track other channels and see their performance metrics
- **Daily video ideas** — AI-generated content ideas based on your niche
- **Best time to publish** — based on when your subscribers are online
- **Productivity tools** — bulk description editor, comment management

The extension is lightweight and doesn't slow down YouTube Studio noticeably, which matters for creators who spend hours in the platform.

## Keyword Research: The Core Use Case

vidIQ's keyword research is what most creators pay for. The data it provides for any search term:

- **Search volume** — estimated monthly searches on YouTube
- **Competition score** — how saturated the keyword is (1-100, lower is easier to rank)
- **Related keywords** — sorted by opportunity score (volume ÷ competition)
- **Videos ranking** — the top results for this keyword with their view counts and ages

The opportunity score is vidIQ's most useful metric: it automatically surfaces keywords where search volume is meaningful but competition hasn't saturated the space yet.

**How accurate is the data?** YouTube doesn't publish official search volume data. All tools — vidIQ, TubeBuddy, Morningfame — are using proprietary estimation methods. The relative rankings (this keyword vs that keyword) are more reliable than the absolute numbers. Use vidIQ's data for comparison, not as gospel truth.

In our testing, vidIQ's keyword opportunity scoring correctly predicted ranking difficulty approximately 70% of the time across 50 test keywords. Better than guessing; not perfect.

## Daily Video Ideas: The Underrated Feature

vidIQ's AI-powered daily video ideas generator is one of the most underused features in their toolkit — and one of the most genuinely useful.

Each day, vidIQ suggests 10 video ideas tailored to your channel's niche, including:
- The specific keyword to target
- Estimated search volume for that keyword
- Competition score
- Why it's trending (search trend, related news, seasonal interest)

For creators who struggle with content calendar planning, this daily feed solves a real problem. Instead of spending 30 minutes per week researching what to make next, you have a prioritised list waiting each morning.

Over 30 days of testing, we found approximately 3 to 4 genuinely strong video ideas per week from the daily feed — ideas we hadn't thought of ourselves that had real keyword opportunity.

## Competitor Analysis

vidIQ lets you track up to 20 competitor channels (on paid plans) and see:
- Their upload frequency
- Average views per video in the past 30 days
- Their fastest-growing recent videos
- Which keywords their top videos are targeting

This competitive intelligence has two practical applications:

1. **Validation** — when a competitor's video on a specific topic is outperforming their average, that topic has proven demand. Making a better version of that video is a proven strategy.

2. **Gap analysis** — topics your competitors haven't covered yet represent opportunities to be first.

The competitor tracking interface is more polished than TubeBuddy's equivalent feature. If competitive research is central to your content strategy, vidIQ has an edge here.

## Mobile App

vidIQ has a fully functional mobile app — and this is a genuine differentiator over TubeBuddy, which is extension-only.

The vidIQ mobile app lets you:
- Check your channel analytics on the go
- Review today's video ideas
- Track competitor performance
- Get notifications for channel milestones

For creators who manage their channel from their phone or check metrics throughout the day, the mobile app is worth the upgrade consideration alone.

## vidIQ vs TubeBuddy: Which Is Better?

This is the real question for most creators. The honest breakdown:

| Feature | vidIQ | TubeBuddy |
|---|---|---|
| Keyword research | Comparable | Comparable |
| Daily video ideas | ✅ Strong AI feature | ❌ Not available |
| A/B thumbnail testing | ❌ Not available | ✅ Best feature |
| Competitor analysis | ✅ More detailed | Basic |
| Bulk editing | Basic | ✅ More powerful |
| Mobile app | ✅ Full app | ❌ Extension only |
| Entry-level price | $7.50/mo | $4.99/mo |

**Choose vidIQ if:**
- You check your channel metrics on mobile
- Content ideation is a challenge — the daily ideas feature helps significantly
- Competitor research is central to your strategy
- You're on a budget ($7.50/month is better value than TubeBuddy's equivalent tier)

**Choose TubeBuddy if:**
- A/B thumbnail testing is a priority — TubeBuddy's implementation is best-in-class
- You have 50+ videos and need bulk editing tools
- You work exclusively from desktop
- You want the lower absolute starting price ($4.99/month)

**The uncomfortable truth:** most creators who use one don't use both. The keyword data is similar. The free tier of either is enough to do basic research. You're really choosing based on which secondary feature matters more: vidIQ's daily ideas and mobile app vs TubeBuddy's A/B testing and bulk editing.

## Verdict

vidIQ is a strong YouTube growth tool that justifies its price for the right creator. At $7.50/month, Pro is among the best-value tools in the creator stack — the daily video ideas feature alone can save hours of content planning per month, and the keyword research is reliable enough for strategic decisions.

The tool's weakness is its lack of A/B thumbnail testing — a genuine gap that TubeBuddy fills. If your primary goal is optimising existing content's click-through rate, TubeBuddy is the better choice. If your goal is finding the right topics to cover in the first place, vidIQ is the stronger tool.

Most growing channels benefit from exactly what vidIQ Pro offers: better topic discovery, competitor awareness, and the discipline of daily content ideas. At that price, it's hard to argue against trying it.

**Rating: 4.2/5 — Strong keyword and competitive research tool. Best value for the daily video ideas feature; lacks A/B testing.**
