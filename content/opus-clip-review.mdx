## What Opus Clip Does

At its core, Opus Clip solves one specific problem: you're producing long-form YouTube content and you're leaving reach on the table by not repurposing it as short-form. Most creators know they should be posting Shorts and Reels, but the manual process of identifying moments, cropping to vertical, adding captions, and scheduling across platforms takes 2 to 3 hours per video.

Opus Clip's AI handles all of that in about 10 minutes.

## Clip Selection Quality

This is the most important question: does the AI find the good moments?

Our honest answer: yes, reliably. Not perfectly, but reliably.

Over 90 days, we ran 34 videos through Opus Clip. For each video, we also had a human editor manually select what they thought were the best clips. We then compared:

- **In 29 out of 34 videos**, at least 2 of the AI's top 3 clips overlapped with the human editor's top 3 choices
- **In 8 videos**, the AI found a clip that the human editor had missed, which ended up being the best performer
- **In 5 videos**, the AI selections were noticeably weaker — mostly in highly technical or slow-paced educational content

The AI is specifically trained to identify moments with high energy, strong hooks, quotable statements, and natural standalone narrative arcs. It performs best on talking-head content and interviews, and worst on screencast tutorials or slow educational explainers.

## The Virality Score

Each clip gets a "Virality Score" between 0 and 100. Opus Clip explains that this score predicts how likely the clip is to perform well based on engagement signals.

**Our results on the Virality Score:** It's a useful ranking tool, not a reliable predictor. We found that Virality Score correlated with actual view count about 60% of the time — better than random selection, but not dramatically so. Treat it as a rough quality signal, not as a promise.

One pattern we noticed: Virality Scores above 80 consistently performed better than clips under 60. The middle range (60 to 80) was where the score was least predictive.

## Auto-Reframe Quality

Opus Clip automatically converts your 16:9 landscape video to 9:16 vertical by tracking the speaker and keeping them centred.

For talking-head content with one speaker: excellent. The tracking is smooth and the speaker stays centred even during animated gestures.

For content with multiple people, or where the camera cuts between different shots: variable. The AI handles most cuts correctly, but occasionally it lags on a sudden camera switch and you get 1 to 2 frames of a badly framed shot.

For screencasts or animated content: Opus Clip adds letterboxing (black bars) which looks acceptable but not ideal. A human editor would do better here.

---

## Caption Quality

Auto-captions are included at all paid tiers. Accuracy is high for clear audio — we measured approximately 97% accuracy on clean ElevenLabs voiceover, and 91 to 95% on human narration with some background noise.

The caption styling is customisable: you can choose from several preset styles (including the bold pop-up style popular on viral content) and adjust colours and position.

One limitation: caption editing requires clicking on individual words in a timeline, which is slower than it should be when you need to fix a batch of errors. A full-text editor for captions would make corrections much faster.
