## The YouTube Creator Workflow — What Can Be Automated?

Before picking tools, map your workflow. Most channels have these recurring tasks:

1. **Topic research** — finding what to make next
2. **Scripting** — writing the video
3. **Recording or generating** — production
4. **Thumbnail creation** — design
5. **Video editing** — cutting and polishing
6. **Publishing** — uploading, titles, descriptions, tags
7. **Repurposing** — turning the video into Shorts, tweets, newsletter

Steps 1, 6, and 7 are the easiest to automate significantly. Steps 2 through 5 can be partially automated. The goal is not to remove yourself from the process — it's to eliminate the mechanical, low-value parts.

## Make (formerly Integromat)

Make is the most powerful general-purpose automation tool available at a price that solo creators can afford. It uses a visual, drag-and-drop scenario builder where you connect "modules" — inputs, transforms, and outputs — to automate complex multi-step workflows.

**What YouTube creators use it for:**

- Auto-publish a new video → trigger a tweet thread, a LinkedIn post, and an email newsletter, all formatted differently for each platform
- Monitor a Google Trends RSS feed → if a trending topic matches your niche keywords → create a draft in Notion with a suggested title and script outline (using a Claude or ChatGPT module)
- When a new video hits 1,000 views → send yourself a Slack notification with the top-performing thumbnail for that video

**The learning curve is real.** Make takes longer to set up than Zapier, but the free plan is significantly more generous and complex logic (filters, routers, iterators) is available at lower price tiers.

## Opus Clip

Opus Clip does one thing and does it exceptionally well: it takes your long-form YouTube video and automatically creates short-form clips for YouTube Shorts, TikTok, and Instagram Reels.

You upload (or paste the URL of) a video, and Opus Clip's AI identifies the best moments, reframes them to vertical 9:16 format, adds auto-captions, and ranks the clips by predicted virality score. The process takes about 10 minutes for a 20-minute video.

**The quality is genuinely impressive.** The AI finds moments that a human editor would also flag — compelling statements, surprising reveals, quotable lines. It's not perfect, but the best 2 or 3 clips it surfaces are usually worth posting.

**The auto-posting feature** lets you schedule clips directly to YouTube Shorts and TikTok from within the app, which eliminates a lot of manual platform management.

## TubeBuddy

TubeBuddy is a browser extension that sits directly inside YouTube Studio and adds AI-powered research and optimisation features. It's the most accessible tool in this list — zero setup, installs in 30 seconds, and you're immediately getting data you didn't have before.

**Most useful features for creators:**

- **Keyword Explorer:** Shows search volume, competition score, and related keywords for any YouTube search term. More accurate for YouTube specifically than general SEO tools.
- **A/B Thumbnail Testing:** Automatically rotates between two thumbnail versions and shows which generates a higher CTR. This single feature can improve a channel's performance significantly.
- **Best Time to Publish:** Shows when your existing subscribers are most active.
- **Bulk Processing:** Update descriptions, end screens, and cards across multiple videos at once — critical when updating links or sponsor calls.

## A Simple Automation Stack for a Faceless Channel

Here's what a solo creator running a faceless YouTube channel realistically needs:

**Research:** TubeBuddy Keyword Explorer + Google Trends (free)

**Scripting:** Claude or ChatGPT with a saved system prompt

**Production:** ElevenLabs (voice) + Kling or Pika (visuals) or a stock footage subscription

**Thumbnail:** Midjourney → Canva

**Repurposing:** Opus Clip for Shorts

**Distribution automation:** Make scenario — new YouTube video published → auto-post to Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Telegram channel

**Analytics:** TubeBuddy inside YouTube Studio

Total monthly cost for this stack: approximately $60 to $90. A single monetised video that performs well pays for several months.

## The One Automation Most Creators Skip

The highest-leverage automation that almost no one sets up: **auto-repurposing of your video description into a newsletter**.

Set up a Make scenario:
1. Trigger: New video published on YouTube
2. Action: Send the video title + description to ChatGPT with a prompt: "Rewrite this as a 200-word email newsletter section introducing this YouTube video, in a casual, direct tone"
3. Action: Add the output as a draft in your email platform (ConvertKit, Beehiiv, etc.)

You review and send in 5 minutes. The alternative is either not emailing your list at all, or spending 30 minutes writing a separate newsletter entry. Over a year of weekly uploads, this saves over 25 hours.
